    Discography

    2016‚Äì2019: Pre-Debut

    Ningning and Karina's First Appearances
    Before the official debut of the group, Ningning was the first to be introduced in 2016 through the SM ROOKIES project, making her the only pre-revealed member of the group.

    Karina made her appearance in 2019 as a backup dancer in the music video for Taemin's title track "Want" from his mini-album and performed briefly on various music shows.

    2020: Official Debut

    Debut
    On October 26, SM Entertainment launched social media accounts for the new girl group. On the 27th, SM Entertainment revealed the first member, Winter, through teaser photos on its official website and social media. Winter was introduced as a member with strong vocal and dance skills.

    On the 28th, SM Entertainment revealed the second member, Karina, highlighting her talents in singing, rapping, and dancing. On the same day, the group's YouTube channel released a teaser video introducing their unique concept and Karina's corresponding virtual counterpart, ae-Karina.

    On the 29th, the third member, Ningning, was introduced, known for her outstanding vocal ability. On the 30th, the fourth member, Giselle, was introduced as a skilled rapper fluent in Korean, Japanese, and English.

    2021: Release of "Forever," "Next Level," and First Mini-Album "Savage"

    "Black Mamba" MV Hits 100 Million Views
    On January 8, the "Black Mamba" music video surpassed 100 million views in just 52 days, becoming aespa's first MV to achieve this milestone and setting a record for the fastest debut MV to reach 100 million views.

    On May 17, aespa released their third single, "Next Level." On June 19, the "Next Level" music video surpassed 100 million views in 33 days, becoming aespa's second MV to achieve this milestone.

    2022: Second Mini-Album "Girls"

    Expansion into the U.S.
    On June 1, SM Entertainment announced a global partnership with Warner Records, which would be responsible for the distribution and promotion of aespa's music. On the same day, the pre-release track "Illusion" was unveiled.

    On June 24, aespa released their English single "Life's Too Short." On July 8, they released their second mini-album, "Girls."

    2023: First World Tour, Third Mini-Album "MY WORLD," Fourth Mini-Album "Drama"

    World Tour
    On February 25 and 26, aespa kicked off their first solo concert tour, "SYNK : HYPER LINE," at Jamsil Indoor Stadium in South Korea.

    On May 8, they released their third mini-album, "MY WORLD," which sold over 1.37 million copies on its first day, setting a record as the highest first-day sales for a girl group album in history. On May 24, aespa, as global brand ambassadors for Chopard, attended the 76th Cannes Film Festival, becoming the first K-pop girl group to appear at the event.

    2024: Single "Zetta," First Full-Length Album "Armageddon," Japanese Debut

    Japanese Debut
    On July 3, aespa officially debuted in Japan with the release of their first Japanese single, "Hot Mess."

    On July 21, the music video for the title track "Supernova" from their full-length album surpassed 100 million views in 69 days, becoming aespa's eighth MV to reach this milestone.

    2025

    New Record
    On January 1, the music video for the title track "Whiplash" from their fifth mini-album surpassed 100 million views in 72 days, becoming aespa's tenth MV to reach this milestone.

    Awards

    Main Article: aespa Award and Nomination List

    As of February 2025, aespa has received over 160 nominations and won over 80 awards, including 14 grand prizes. At the 2024 Melon Music Awards, aespa swept the Artist of the Year, Album of the Year, and Song of the Year awards, becoming the only K-POP girl group to achieve this feat. With the Producer of the Year award in 2021, aespa became the second group, after BTS, to win four grand prizes at the Melon Music Awards and the only girl group to do so.

    In terms of music show wins, aespa has secured 37 number one spots.
